PROFILE




Sadia Ilyas is a Brain Pool Scientist, National Research Foundation of Korea (NRF) and a Professor (Research) in the Department of Earth Resources & Environmental Engineering at Hanyang University (Seoul), South Korea. She has a strong background in inorganic chemistry and earned her Ph.D. in 2011 with doctoral research at the University of Agriculture Faisalabad focused on the metals-to-microbes interactions in the geo-environment and their application in sustainable exploitation of valuable metals from the burgeoning legacy of digital world (i.e., electronic waste).


Dr. Ilyas’s research has taken her to different international platforms at various capacities in both academia and industrial R&D. Before joining as a Professor (Research) at Hanyang University (Seoul main campus in Korea), she has worked as an Associate Professor (Research) at Jeonbuk National University (Jeonju, Korea), Assistant Professor at University of Agriculture (Faisalabad, Pakistan), Postdoc at Korea Institute of Geoscience and Research and Mineral Resources (Daejeon, Korea), Sr. Researcher at School of Chemical Engineering and Pharmacy of Wuhan Institute of Technology (Wuhan, China), and Lecturer at GC University of Faisalabad (Faisalabad, Pakistan).


Dr. Ilyas is also an independent consultant in the field of hydrometallurgy that includes bio-hydrometallurgy as well and transferred several technologies at industrial level. She has 5 patents, published around 100 research articles in peer-reviewed journals, 4 books, 40 contributed book chapters, and many conference papers, besides several invited talks at international institutions. She has supervised 45 M.Sc., 41 M.Phil. and 2 Ph.D. students as main supervisor, and 46 M.Phil. students supervised as member of supervisory committee during last five years. She has remined associated with the Biochemical Society (UK), The Chemical Society of Pakistan, Korean Society of Metal (Korea), Korean Resource Recycling Society (Korea), United State-Pakistan center for advanced studies in agriculture and food security (USPCAS-AFS) for UAF and UC Davis, Screening and evaluation committee (biotechnology) of U.S.-Pakistan center for advance studies, etc.
